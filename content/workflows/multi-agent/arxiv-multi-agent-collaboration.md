## Abstract

With recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs), Agentic AI has become phenomenal in real-world applications, moving toward multiple LLM-based agents to perceive, learn, reason, and act collaboratively. These LLM-based Multi-Agent Systems (MASs) enable groups of intelligent agents to coordinate and solve complex tasks collectively at scale, transitioning from isolated models to collaboration-centric approaches.

This work provides an extensive survey of the collaborative aspect of MASs and introduces an extensible framework to guide future research. Our framework characterizes collaboration mechanisms based on key dimensions:

- **Actors**: agents involved
- **Types**: cooperation, competition, or coopetition
- **Structures**: peer-to-peer, centralized, or distributed
- **Strategies**: role-based or model-based
- **Coordination protocols**

Through a review of existing methodologies, our findings serve as a foundation for demystifying and advancing LLM-based MASs toward more intelligent and collaborative solutions for complex, real-world use cases.

## Application Domains

The paper investigates MASs across diverse domains:
- 5G/6G networks
- Industry 5.0
- Question answering
- Social and cultural settings

## Key Research Contributions

1. **Extensible Framework**: A comprehensive framework characterizing collaboration mechanisms across five key dimensions
2. **Survey Analysis**: Review of existing methodologies for LLM-based multi-agent systems
3. **Application Survey**: Investigation of MASs across multiple real-world domains
4. **Future Directions**: Identification of open challenges and potential research directions toward artificial collective intelligence

## Key Lessons and Open Challenges

The paper identifies key lessons learned and open challenges in MAS research, including:
- Effective coordination protocols for large-scale agent systems
- Scaling challenges as agent count increases
- Communication overhead and efficiency
- Robustness to agent failures
- Alignment and safety in collaborative settings
